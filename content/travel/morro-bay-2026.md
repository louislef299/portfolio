Welp, everything I read was true. The California surf in the summer is very mild
relative to the winters. After a short hour-and-a-half surf session yesterday
with some shoulder-height sets rolling in, the fog got thick and the wind
direction shifted, prompting an end to the day.

This morning, I woke up after snoozing my alarm for an hour and checked [the
surfcam][Morro SurfCam]. Pretty tame. Should rent a longboard or something.
Anyways, even with the tamer weather, I think Elise has enjoyed the focus not
just surfing the entire vacation. And don't get me wrong, there is still [plenty
to do in SloCal][Discover SloCal], and we have plans on exploring Los Osos and
Paso Robles in the coming days.

While I'm sitting here sipping my coffee and waking up slowly, I'm also
researching which wetsuit to finally pull trigger on this fall to surf Superior.
I'm eying [Patagonia's R5][], which is sitting at a cool $639, so I'll have to
save up for that. I don't mind shelling out for a wetsuit that will need to keep
me from the brutal cold of Superior though.

This was just a short post. We'll see if I get around to making a longer post
later, but Elise has been taking some great photos, so I'll be updating the vsco
at the very least 🌸
